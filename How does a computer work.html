How does a computer work?

Back to home page

It is by no means necessary to be familiar with the ins and outs of a computer to be able to use it, but it's good to have a basic understanding of them.

To start, it's good to mention that a computer is made up of a lot more than what one sees at first glance. The "brain" of a computer is found in its CPU (central processing unit) and it interprets commands through binary code, ones and zeroes.

Picture of a cpu. It's rather small for all it does.

What you, the user, are looking at when using a computer is merely a monitor and an interface used to control the computer. This interface is known as the "operating system".
